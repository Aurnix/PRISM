# PRISM Demo Scenario — Fictional Product Definition
## "Ledgerflow" — AI-Native Accounting Automation Platform

**Purpose:** This document defines the fictional product that PRISM is being demonstrated as if built for. All demo dossiers, ICP scoring, outreach angles, and play selection are generated from this product's perspective.

**Note:** Ledgerflow is fictional. It's designed to be realistic and representative of the AI-native fintech category (comparable to Rillet, Puzzle, Digits, or Numeric).

---

## Product Overview

**Ledgerflow** is an AI-native accounting automation platform built for high-growth SaaS and technology companies outgrowing QuickBooks.

**One-liner:** "The accounting platform that closes your books while you sleep."

**What it does:**
- **Automated month-end close:** Reduces close timelines from 15-20 business days to 3-5 by automating reconciliation, journal entries, and variance analysis
- **ASC 606 revenue recognition:** Automated revenue recognition for SaaS subscription models with complex billing (usage-based, multi-year contracts, bundles)
- **Multi-entity consolidation:** Unified financial view across subsidiaries, international entities, and business units without manual spreadsheet consolidation
- **Real-time financial reporting:** Live dashboards replacing the "wait until month-end to know where we stand" problem
- **AI-powered anomaly detection:** Flags unusual transactions, missing accruals, and reconciliation discrepancies before they become audit findings
- **Audit-ready output:** Generates audit workpapers, supporting schedules, and SOC 2-aligned documentation automatically

**What it integrates with:**
- **Banking:** Plaid-based bank feeds (all major US banks)
- **Payments:** Stripe, Brex, Ramp, Mercury, SVB
- **Billing:** Stripe Billing, Chargebee, Recurly, Zuora
- **HRIS/Payroll:** Rippling, Gusto, Deel
- **ERP migration:** Import from QuickBooks Online, QuickBooks Desktop, Xero, and early-stage NetSuite
- **Data warehouse:** Snowflake, BigQuery, Redshift (for custom reporting)

**How it's different from QuickBooks/Xero:**
- Built for SaaS revenue recognition complexity, not general small business accounting
- AI-native (not AI bolted onto legacy architecture)
- Scales from Series A to Series D without needing to migrate to NetSuite
- API-first — engineers can build on top of it

**How it's different from NetSuite:**
- 10x faster implementation (days, not months)
- No consultants or implementation partners required
- Modern UX that accountants actually enjoy using
- Fraction of the cost at the growth stage
- No "shelfware" — you use all of it from day one

**How it's different from Rillet/Puzzle/Digits:**
- Stronger AI capabilities in anomaly detection and automated journal entries
- Superior multi-entity consolidation (competitor weakness)
- Purpose-built for ASC 606 edge cases (usage-based billing, milestone recognition)
- Self-serve implementation with AI-guided migration

## Ideal Customer Profile (for demo purposes)

**Primary ICP:**
- SaaS or technology company
- Series A through Series D ($5M-$200M raised)
- 50-500 employees
- $5M-$100M ARR
- Currently on QuickBooks, Xero, or early NetSuite (or spreadsheets)
- US-headquartered (GAAP accounting)
- Experiencing at least one of:
  - Month-end close taking >10 business days
  - Revenue recognition complexity (usage-based, multi-year, bundles)
  - Multi-entity or international entity complexity
  - Upcoming audit or SOC 2 requirement
  - Board or investor pressure for faster/better financial reporting

**Primary buyer personas:**

| Persona | Title | What They Care About | How They Talk |
|---------|-------|---------------------|---------------|
| **Champion** | VP Finance / Controller | Month-end close time, team efficiency, accuracy, career impact | Operational, specific, metrics-focused |
| **Economic Buyer** | CFO / CEO | Cost of current approach, audit readiness, board confidence, time-to-close reduction | Strategic, ROI-focused, risk-aware |
| **Technical Gatekeeper** | CTO / VP Engineering | API quality, data model, integration architecture, security, build-vs-buy | Technical, skeptical, wants documentation |
| **User** | Staff Accountant / Senior Accountant | Daily workflow pain, manual reconciliation burden, month-end stress | Practical, frustrated, wants things to "just work" |

## Competitive Landscape (for demo purposes)

**Direct competitors:**
- **Rillet:** Closest competitor. Strong on core accounting, weaker on multi-entity. VC-backed (Sequoia). Ledgerflow differentiates on AI capabilities and ASC 606 depth.
- **Puzzle:** Targets earlier stage (Seed/Series A). Less robust for scaling companies. Ledgerflow wins at Series B+.
- **Digits:** More focused on financial reporting/dashboards than core accounting automation. Complementary in some cases.
- **Numeric:** Close analytics and reporting layer. Not a full accounting platform.

**Category competitors (what they're replacing):**
- **QuickBooks Online:** The default. Everyone starts here. Migration from QBO is Ledgerflow's primary acquisition motion.
- **NetSuite:** The "enterprise" option growth companies migrate to. Expensive, slow to implement, requires consultants. Ledgerflow positions as "NetSuite-grade capabilities without the NetSuite pain."
- **Spreadsheets:** Many companies supplement QBO with extensive spreadsheet processes. The spreadsheet is the real competitor.
